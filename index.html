Definition of NLP

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a sub-field of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that is focused on enabling computers to understand and process human languages, to get computers closer to a human level of understanding language. Artificial Intelligence is changing the way we look at the world surrounded by machine learning. Google, Netflix, data companies, video games and more use AI to comb through large amount of data. NLP draws from many disciplines, including computer science and computational linguistics, in its pursuit to fill the gap between human communication and computer understanding.

NLP is a form of Artificial Intelligence that analyzes the human language. Technologies help machines understand and even communicate with human speech. These underlying tasks are often used in higher-level NLP capabilities, such as:

  • Content categorization. A linguistic-based document summary, including search and indexing, content alerts and duplication detection.
  • Topic discovery and modeling. Accurately capture the meaning and themes in text collections, and apply advanced analytics to text, like optimization and forecasting.
  • Contextual extraction. Automatically pull structured information from text-based sources.
  • Sentiment analysis. Identifying the mood or subjective opinions within large amounts of text, including average sentiment and opinion mining. 
  • Speech-to-text and text-to-speech conversion. Transforming voice commands into written text, and vice versa. 
  • Document summarization. Automatically generating synopses of large bodies of text.
  • Machine translation. Automatic translation of text or speech from one language to another.

In all these cases, the overarching goal is to take raw language input and use linguistics and algorithms to transform or enrich the text in such a way that it delivers greater value.

NLP depends on the application of the system. Voice-based systems like Alexa or Google Assistant translate your words into text using Hidden Markov Models system (HMM).

  • HMM uses math model to determine what you’ve said and translate it into text.
  • The system breaks word down into parts of speech (through a series of coded grammar rules that rely on algorithms).
  • The ability to categorize what is said in different ways.

How NLP relates to healthcare

Technology and Artificial Intelligence has allowed healthcare to expand and improve how care is given to patients. Natural Language processing is one of the many newer IT tools that can help simplify the process of care given to patients by their physicians. Currently, NLP uses in healthcare include:

  1. IMPROVING THE PRECISION AND COMPREHENSIVENESS OF THE ELECTRONIC HEALTH RECORD

    2. NLP software is able to take spoken or written data and transcribe it for the use of billing, authorizations, clinical decision support and more. NLP software allows the provider to verbally dictate their notes while it is electronically transcribed quality care with the added bonus of the patient also being involved in the process. This permits the patient to hear what their physician is dictating and they can add to the conversation that is being held about their health.

  2. CLINICAL DECISION SUPPORT

    3. A major concern in healthcare is preventative care especially for those with severe health complications. One way to combat this area of concern is to apply NLP algorithms that search for unstructured data. This includes social, behavioral, and other health risk factors all found in a patient’s EHR. An example of this is IBM Watson, a natural language processing machine that was able to identify 8,500 patients at risk of developing heart disease within a year. Today NLP’s role in risk stratifications is used by clinical research facilities, academia, and government health sectors to name a few.

  3. IDENTIFYING ERRORS IN THE CARE PATIENTS ARE RECEIVING

    4. Healthcare providers strive to give efficient care however sometimes mistakes are made. Fortunately, NLP can measure a physician’s performance of care and any errors that may have occurred in the process can hypothetically be identified. For example, an algorithm can be designed to distinguish if appropriate meds have been authorized to patients with an illness or disease. This helps give the patient quality care while also cutting back on unnecessary medical costs.

Uses of NLP in Healthcare

  • ⇒ To provide more patient-provider interaction and to lessen worries about burdensome documentation.
  • ⇒ Used as an alternative way to typing or hand-writing clinical notes.
  • ⇒ To help make more effective clinical decision support.
  • ⇒ It has the ability to transform big data into smart data.
  • ⇒ It has the ability to analyze and extract meaning from narrative text or other unstructured data sources.
  • ⇒ Can enhance the completeness and accuracy of electronic health records.
  • ⇒ Many natural language processing systems has the ability to “learn” over time, reabsorbing previous interactions with other patients where results were accurate but did not meet expectations.
  • ⇒ These machines operate based on statistical probabilities.

Pros:

⇒ Reduces EHR frustration regarding documentation and to provide a stronger patient-doctor interaction.

⇒ It follows a more cooperative approach where the physician can dictate out loud as it is being recorded into the patients EHR as the patient can confirm if the information is being correctly documented.

⇒ Applying NLP to EHR data will help integrate results into the patient portal that will help them understand their health information better.

  • Having the ability to translate free text to standardized data
  • Able to link medical terms to simple definitions to improve patient EHR understanding and patient portal experience
  • NLP essentially will work as a virtual assistant to aid with administration duties as well as customer service and help desk tasks

⇒ NLP algorithms are developed to help extract relevant EHR data that can help identify potential errors in care delivery or in helping detect complex patients who may benefit from enhanced care coordination.

  • One research team applied an NLP tool to unstructured data to identify adverse drug events in media literature and social media postings
  • Achieved 92.7% accuracy & 93.6% precision
  • NLP algorithms can also offer a solution that can identify key terms associated with the social determinants of health
  • 2018 algorithm used to help providers measure the quality of inpatient care and monitor adherence to clinical guidelines

Cons:

⇒ Speech recognition software prone to leaving errors in EHR that can put patients at significant risk of harm.

  • Largest portion of errors were related to medications while surgical notes had the fewest

⇒ Solutions to speech recognition/natural language processing errors:

  • Human transcriptionist to review information
  • Users may need coaching on how to best phrase key elements of the clinical notes and which types of sounds or words are most likely to be mistranslated and how to adequately train NLP software to recognize a unique accent or speech pattern
  • Clinical assurance and auditing programs and awareness of these errors to come up with strategies to reduce them

⇒ Narrative Text and “doctor speak” – word disambiguation or fragmented words makes up at least a third of clinical errors due to certain abbreviations having multiple meanings.

  • ex) discharge – bodily secretion or being released from hospital, MD – doctor of medicine or mental disorder

⇒ Humans can usually decipher abbreviations and such due to context and other surrounding information, NLP technology still has a way to go before it can have that level of reliability.

⇒ Eventually NLP tools will be able to bridge that gap between the amount of data being generated on a daily basis to piece certain words to context.

NLP in Clinical Decision Support

  • ⇒ In 2013, the Department of Veterans Affairs used NLP techniques to review more than 2 billion EHR documents for indications of PTSD, depression, and potential self-harm in veteran patients.  The pilot was 80 percent accurate at identifying the difference between records of screenings for suicide and mentions of actual past suicide attempts.
  • ⇒ Researchers at MIT in 2012 were able to attain a 75 percent accuracy rate for deciphering the semantic meaning of specific clinical terms contained in free-text clinical notes, using a statistical probability model to assess surrounding terms and put ambiguous terms into context. 
  • ⇒ Natural language processing was able to take the speech patterns of schizophrenic patients and identify which were likely to experience an onset of psychosis with 100 percent accuracy.  The small proof-of-concept study employed an NLP system with “a novel combination of semantic coherence and syntactic assays as predictors of psychosis transition.”
  • ⇒ At the University of California Los Angeles, researchers analyzed electronic free text to flag patients with cirrhosis.  By combining natural language processing of radiology reports with ICD-9 codes and lab data, the algorithm attained incredibly high levels of sensitivity and specificity.
  • ⇒ Researchers from the University of Alabama found that NLP identification of reportable cancer cases was 22.6 percent more accurate and precise than manual review of medical records.  The system helped to separate cancer patients whose conditions should be reported to the Cancer Registry Control Panel from cases that did not have to be included in the registry.
  • ⇒ Intermountain has used NLP to identify stroke patients, cancer patients, heart failure patients and patients with venous thromboembolisms.
    • Ex; Heart failure patients often will come into the hospital for a reason other than their heart failure problems. By using NLP, Intermountain found that they could identify heart failure patients out of reading data from 25 different types of free text documents stored in the electronic health record.
